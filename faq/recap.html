01 · OVERVIEW

The Basics — What Is Recap?

9 questions

Recap is a meeting transcription and intelligence tool that runs entirely on your device. It captures audio from your meetings (Zoom, Teams, Google Meet, or any other platform), transcribes the conversation locally, and generates structured summaries with action items.

Here's how it works:

  1. Audio capture: Recap captures system audio (what you hear through your speakers/headphones) — no cloud bots joining your meetings
  2. Transcription: Audio is transcribed locally using Whisper (open-source speech recognition)
  3. Summarization: AI generates a summary, extracts action items, and identifies decisions
  4. Organization: Transcripts and summaries are stored locally, searchable, and exportable

The key difference from other meeting tools: everything happens on your device. No audio or transcripts are sent to the cloud. Full sovereignty.

Organizations face a tension between two needs:

  • Workflow automation: Cloud-based meeting tools (Otter.ai, Fireflies, Grain) are convenient but require sending your meeting audio to their servers
  • Data sovereignty: Regulated industries, privacy-conscious teams, and organizations in data-residency contexts can't send sensitive conversations to third-party servers

Recap solves this by providing on-device meeting intelligence. Your audio and transcripts never leave your machine. No cloud bots silently joining calls. No transcripts sitting on a vendor's servers.

The resultYou get the benefits of meeting intelligence (summaries, action items, searchable transcripts) without sacrificing data sovereignty.

Recap is designed for regulated and privacy-conscious organizations:

  • Legal firms: Attorney-client privilege requires keeping meeting conversations confidential
  • Healthcare: HIPAA-adjacent workflows where patient discussions can't be sent to cloud services
  • Financial services: Fintech and financial institutions with strict data controls
  • Government contractors: Defense-adjacent SMBs handling sensitive government data
  • EU-based organizations: GDPR data-minimization requirements
  • Any organization: Where "no cloud bot joining our call" is a hard requirement

If you can't use Otter.ai or Fireflies because of data sovereignty concerns, Recap is for you.

  • Word Error Rate (WER): ≤8% on standard business English meetings (measured against human transcription baseline)

Accuracy depends on:

  • Model size: Larger models (Whisper large-v3) are more accurate but slower
  • Language: English is best supported (v1.0); other languages coming in v1.1

Recap generates structured summaries:

  • Key topics: List of main topics discussed
  • Decisions: List of decisions made during the meeting
  • Action items: List of action items with assignees and deadlines (if stated)
  • Key quotes: Important quotes from the meeting

Summaries are generated using local LLMs (e.g., Llama 3, Mistral) — no cloud processing required.

CustomizableYou can customize summary templates (e.g., focus on decisions, action items, or specific topics).

Recap uses AI-powered extraction to identify action items:

  • Keyword detection: Identifies phrases like "I'll do X", "Can you handle Y?", "Let's follow up on Z"
  • Assignee detection: Identifies who is responsible for each action item
  • Deadline detection: Identifies deadlines if stated (e.g., "by Friday", "next week")
  • Context understanding: Understands the context to distinguish actual action items from general discussion

Target: ≥85% precision, ≥75% recall (human-evaluated sample).

Action items are extracted locally using LLMs — no cloud processing required.

Scaling & Operations

7 questions

Recap can handle unlimited meetings:

  • No meeting limit: Transcribe as many meetings as you want
  • No time limit: Transcribe meetings of any duration
  • Storage: Limited only by your disk space (typical meeting = 50-200 MB)

For large volumes (100+ meetings per week), we recommend:

  • Sufficient disk space: Ensure you have enough storage for audio and transcripts
  • Batch mode: Transcribe after meetings end (faster, more accurate)
  • Team deployment: Use a central server for team access (paid tier)

Recap supports unlimited users:

  • Free tier: Single-user per device (each user has their own local data)
  • Pro tier: Team workspaces with multiple users
  • Enterprise tier: Centralized server, unlimited users

For team deployments, Recap scales horizontally — add more server resources to handle more users.

If Recap crashes, your audio is not lost:

  • Audio buffer: Recap buffers audio locally — if it crashes, the buffered audio is saved
  • Auto-restart: Recap automatically restarts and resumes capturing audio
  • Manual recovery: You can manually recover the buffered audio and transcribe it later

For critical meetings, we recommend:

  • Test before: Test Recap before important meetings
  • Backup recording: Use a backup recording method (e.g., Zoom's built-in recording) for critical meetings

Recap includes a performance dashboard:

  • System health: CPU, memory, disk usage
  • Transcription metrics: Number of meetings transcribed, average transcription time, accuracy estimates
  • Model metrics: Which models are being used, resource usage
  • Storage metrics: Disk space used by meetings, transcripts, and models

For team deployments, the admin dashboard provides additional metrics (user activity, workspace usage, etc.).

Recap data is stored locally on your device. Back up using standard backup tools:

  • macOS: Time Machine, or manually copy the Recap data folder
  • Windows: File History, or manually copy the Recap data folder
  • Linux: rsync, or manually copy the Recap data folder

Recap data location:

  • macOS: ~/Library/Application Support/Recap/
  • Windows: %APPDATA%\Recap\
  • Linux: ~/.config/Recap/

We recommend backing up this folder regularly (e.g., daily via Time Machine or cloud sync).
