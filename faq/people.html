01 · OVERVIEW

The Basics — What Is People?

9 questions

People is an AI-powered hiring and HR assistant. It automates the administrative burden of recruiting — sourcing, screening, scheduling, candidate communication — while keeping all hiring decisions in human hands.

Here's how it works:

  1. Post jobs: Create and distribute job postings to multiple boards
  2. Screen candidates: AI parses resumes, matches against role requirements, ranks candidates
  3. Schedule interviews: Automated scheduling with calendar integration
  4. Communicate: AI drafts candidate emails (acknowledgments, rejections, next steps) — human reviews before sending
  5. Manage pipeline: Visual pipeline with collaboration, feedback, and audit trails

The key difference from other ATS tools: audit-ready by design, self-hosted, human-in-the-loop. Every action is logged, all candidate data stays on your infrastructure, and no AI output becomes a hiring decision without human review.

Hiring is simultaneously critical and operationally painful:

  • Admin burden: Recruiters spend most of their time on scheduling, status updates, organizing candidate info — not on human judgment
  • Regulatory tightening: EU AI Act classifies hiring AI as high-risk. GDPR, EEOC, NYC Local Law 144, Illinois BIPA create new audit and bias-testing obligations
  • Cloud risk: Most recruiting tools route applicant data through vendor infrastructure — creating compliance exposure
  • Opaque AI: Many tools offer black-box scoring with no audit trails

People solves this by providing self-hosted, audit-ready, human-in-the-loop hiring automation. You get the efficiency gains of AI without the compliance risk.

People is designed for growing businesses (50–500 employees) in regulated industries:

  • Financial services: Banks, fintech, insurance — strict hiring compliance
  • Healthcare: Hospitals, clinics, health tech — HIPAA-adjacent workflows
  • Government contractors: Defense, federal — strict data sovereignty
  • Legal: Law firms, compliance — attorney-client privilege
  • Education: Universities, edtech — FERPA compliance
  • Any regulated business: Where compliance failures carry existential risk

If you can't afford a compliance failure during hiring, People is for you.

Greenhouse and Lever are cloud-based ATS tools. People is a self-hosted, audit-ready ATS.

"Audit-ready by design" means compliance is architectural, not an afterthought:

  • Every action logged: System actions, human decisions, AI recommendations — all immutably logged with timestamps, actor identification, and rationale
  • Exportable: Generate audit reports in PDF, JSON, CSV for regulatory submissions
  • Tamper-evident: Logs are hash-chained — any tampering is detectable
  • Complete: 100% of system actions are logged — no gaps

When an auditor asks "Show me the decision trail for candidate X," you can generate a complete report in <5 minutes.

Key differenceMost ATS tools treat compliance as a feature to add later. People treats it as the foundation.

"Human-in-the-loop" means no AI output becomes a hiring decision without human review:

  • AI assists: AI ranks candidates, drafts emails, suggests next steps
  • Humans decide: A human must review and approve before any action is taken
  • Override tracked: When humans override AI recommendations, it's logged (used to improve the system)

This is an architectural constraint, not a feature. People literally cannot make hiring decisions without human approval.

Why this mattersThe EU AI Act and emerging regulations require human oversight for high-risk AI systems. People's architecture ensures compliance by design.

People offers three deployment options:

  1. Docker Compose: Easiest. One command (docker-compose up) and you're running. Best for development and small deployments.

11 · ROADMAP

Limitations & Roadmap

8 questions

People is powerful, but it has limitations in v1.0:

  • No autonomous decisions: People will not make offer/reject decisions without human approval (architectural constraint)
  • No video interview analysis: No facial recognition, voice analysis, or automated video scoring (regulatory risk too high)
  • No payroll/compensation: People hands off to HRIS at offer acceptance
  • No employee lifecycle: Onboarding, performance reviews, offboarding are out of scope
  • No background checks: People integrates with background check providers but doesn't perform checks
  • No visa/immigration: Out of scope; integration with specialized providers only
  • No native mobile app: Web-responsive design only for v1.0
  • English-only: Multi-language candidate communication planned for v1.1
