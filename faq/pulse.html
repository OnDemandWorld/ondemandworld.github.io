01 · OVERVIEW

The Basics — What Is Pulse?

9 questions

Pulse is a multilingual content generation and localization tool. It helps you create marketing and business content in 50+ languages with genuine cultural adaptation — not just literal translation.

Here's how it works:

  1. You write content in your language: Blog post, ad copy, product description, email campaign, etc.
  2. Pulse adapts it for other markets: Adjusts tone, idiom, formality, cultural references for each target language
  3. Output reads as natively authored: Not translated — localized. A Japanese reader won't feel like they're reading translated English.

The key difference from translation tools: Pulse doesn't just translate words — it adapts culture. Idioms, humor, formality conventions, seasonal references — all adjusted for each market.

Businesses expanding across markets face a localization bottleneck:

  • Literal translation fails culturally: Machine translation produces grammatically correct but culturally tone-deaf output. A campaign that resonates in the US may fall flat or offend in Japan.
  • Brand voice fragments across languages: Maintaining consistent brand personality across 10+ languages requires native-speaking copywriters in each market — most SMBs can't afford this.
  • Third-party cloud dependency creates risk: Sending unreleased campaign copy through third-party APIs introduces IP exposure and regulatory risk.
  • Localization teams are expensive and slow: Professional agencies charge per-word, have multi-day turnaround, and struggle with the pace of modern content marketing.

Pulse solves this by providing culturally-aware, self-hosted content localization. Your brand material stays on your infrastructure. Output is fast, consistent, and culturally adapted.

Pulse is designed for SMBs (10–500 employees) expanding across international markets:

  • E-commerce: Product descriptions, marketing campaigns in multiple languages
  • SaaS companies: Landing pages, blog posts, documentation for global audiences
  • Marketing agencies: Managing multiple client brands across markets
  • Content creators: Blogs, newsletters, social media for international audiences
  • Any business: Producing regular marketing content and need it in multiple languages

If you're producing content and need it in 5+ languages, but can't afford dedicated localization teams in each market, Pulse is for you.

"Cultural adaptation" means adjusting content for each market's cultural norms:

  • Tone: Formal vs. casual, authoritative vs. playful
  • Idiom: Using local expressions, not literal translations
  • Humor: What's funny in one culture may not be in another
  • Formality: Japanese business communication is very formal; Australian is casual
  • Cultural references: Seasonal references, holidays, local customs
  • Regulatory phrasing: Different markets have different compliance language

Example: A US marketing campaign might say "Black Friday deals!" — Pulse adapts this for Japan as "年末セール！" (Year-End Sale!) with appropriate formality and cultural context.

Key differenceTranslation converts words. Localization converts meaning and culture.
