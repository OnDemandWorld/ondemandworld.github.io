01 · OVERVIEW

The Basics — What Is Voice?

9 questions

Voice is an AI phone receptionist. When someone calls your business phone number, Voice answers — just like a human receptionist would. It greets the caller, understands what they need, answers questions from your knowledge base, books appointments, and transfers to a human when needed.

The caller hears a natural, human-sounding voice — not a robotic IVR ("Press 1 for sales, Press 2 for support"). It's a real conversation.

And it works 24/7/365. No sick days, no lunch breaks, no missed calls.

Missed calls cost businesses real money. Industry estimates suggest 20-40% of inbound calls to small and medium businesses go unanswered. Every missed call is a lost appointment, a lost sale, or a lost referral.

Voice solves three problems:

  1. Revenue leakage: Every call is answered. No more voicemail tag. No more "sorry, we missed your call" callbacks.
  2. Staffing cost: A full-time receptionist costs $30K-$50K+/year. Voice handles the same workload for a fraction of the cost — and never takes a day off.
  3. After-hours gaps: Most businesses can't staff phones after 6pm or on weekends. Voice can — 24/7.
Real-world impactAn HVAC company with 6 technicians reported 40% more jobs booked from previously-missed calls after deploying Voice. In peak summer, every missed call is $500-$2,000 in lost revenue.

Here's what Voice handles on a typical call:

  • Answer & greet: Picks up within 3 rings with a natural greeting ("Hi, thanks for calling Westside Dermatology. This is Ava. How can I help?")
  • Appointment booking: Checks your calendar, proposes available slots, confirms the booking, sends a confirmation SMS/email
  • Q&A from your knowledge base: Answers questions about your services, hours, pricing, policies, location, insurance accepted — anything in your documents
  • Call routing: Transfers to the right person or department based on the caller's need
  • Urgent call handling: Detects urgency and pages the on-call person immediately
  • Voicemail capture: If the issue can't be resolved and no human is available, takes a detailed message
  • After-hours handling: Different behavior for business hours vs. after-hours vs. holidays

The voice sounds natural — most callers won't realize it's AI unless they ask. However, we recommend transparency:

  • You can configure the greeting to say "I'm the AI assistant" if you prefer honesty upfront
  • If a caller asks "Are you a robot?", Voice will answer honestly
  • If a caller requests a human, Voice transfers immediately — no deception

The goal isn't to trick callers — it's to give them a great experience. If they want a human, they get one. If they're happy with the AI, they get their issue resolved faster.

02 · SOVEREIGNTY

How It Sounds & Feels

8 questions

Voice is designed for low-latency conversation:

  • Call answer time: Under 800ms (half a second) from when the phone starts ringing
  • Response latency: Under 900ms (typical) from when the caller stops speaking to when Voice starts responding
  • 95th percentile: Under 1.5 seconds even in worst-case scenarios

This is fast enough to feel natural. For comparison, human receptionists typically take 1-3 seconds to respond. Voice is actually faster than most humans.

Why latency mattersLong pauses make conversations feel robotic. Sub-second latency makes Voice feel like talking to a real person.

Yes. Voice supports barge-in (interruption handling). If the caller starts speaking while Voice is talking, Voice stops immediately and listens to the caller.

This is critical for natural conversation. Humans interrupt each other all the time — Voice handles it gracefully.

Voice uses high-quality neural text-to-speech (TTS) — the same technology used by premium voice assistants. The voices sound natural, with proper intonation, pacing, and emotion.

You can choose from a library of preset voices:

  • Male and female voices
  • Different accents (US, UK, Australian)
  • Different tones (friendly, professional, warm, authoritative)
LimitationCustom voice cloning (creating a voice that sounds like a specific person) is planned for v1.2, not available in v1.0.

Voice uses state-of-the-art speech recognition that handles a wide range of accents, dialects, and speech patterns. It's trained on diverse datasets covering:

  • Regional accents (Southern US, British regional, Australian, etc.)
  • Non-native English speakers
  • Background noise (call centers, streets, cars)
  • Fast or slow speech

If Voice can't understand the caller after multiple attempts, it will:

  1. Politely ask the caller to repeat or rephrase
  2. Offer to transfer to a human
  3. Take a detailed voicemail

Voice never pretends to understand when it doesn't.

Voice handles silence and ambiguity gracefully:

  • Short pause (2-3 seconds): Voice waits — the caller may be thinking or looking something up
  • Longer pause (5+ seconds): Voice gently prompts: "Are you still there?" or "Is there anything else I can help with?"
  • No response: After multiple prompts, Voice offers to take a message or end the call politely

This prevents awkward dead air and ensures the caller doesn't feel abandoned.

Yes. Voice includes noise suppression and echo cancellation to handle real-world call conditions:

  • Background noise (traffic, crowds, machinery)
  • Echo (speakerphone, car Bluetooth)
  • Low-quality phone connections

The speech recognition model is trained on noisy data and can extract speech even in challenging conditions. If the audio quality is too poor, Voice will ask the caller to call back from a quieter location or offer to continue via text (SMS/chat).

  • Proper intonation and emotion
  • Natural pacing and pauses
  • Conversational fillers ("um," "let me check") when appropriate
  • Ability to express empathy ("I'm sorry to hear that")

Most callers can't tell the difference between Voice and a human receptionist — especially on a phone call where audio quality is limited.

04 · SECURITY

Accuracy & Trust

7 questions

Voice uses state-of-the-art speech recognition with typical accuracy of 95%+ for clear speech in English. Accuracy depends on:

  • Audio quality: Clear phone connections → higher accuracy. Noisy environments → lower accuracy.
  • Accent: Common accents (US, UK, Australian) → 95%+. Rare or heavy accents → 85-90%.
  • Speech clarity: Clear, moderate-paced speech → higher accuracy. Mumbling or very fast speech → lower accuracy.

If Voice can't understand the caller, it asks for clarification or offers to transfer to a human. It never guesses when it's unsure.

Voice uses retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) — it answers from your documents, not from memory:

  1. Caller asks a question
  2. Voice searches your knowledge base (Vault) for relevant documents
  3. Voice generates an answer grounded in those documents
  4. If the documents don't contain the answer, Voice says "I'm not sure" and offers to transfer

Voice never makes up information. If it doesn't know, it says so.

Confidence thresholdYou can configure a confidence threshold. If Voice's confidence in its answer is below the threshold, it automatically transfers to a human — no guessing.

Voice includes multiple safeguards:

  1. Confirmation: Before booking, Voice reads back the details: "So that's Thursday at 2pm with Dr. Patel. Is that correct?" The caller confirms or corrects.
  2. Post-call summary: After every call, Voice generates a structured summary (intent, outcome, key details). Your team can review these summaries and catch errors.
  3. Human override: If a caller requests a human, Voice transfers immediately. The human can correct any mistakes.
  4. Audit trail: Every call is logged (transcript, recording, structured data). If something goes wrong, you can review exactly what happened.

Mistakes are rare, but when they happen, they're caught and corrected quickly.

Yes. Every call generates:

  • Transcript: Full text of the conversation (caller + Voice)
  • Recording: Audio recording of the call (optional, consent-aware)
  • Structured summary: Intent, outcome, key entities (name, phone, appointment time, etc.)

All of this is available in the admin console. You can:

  • Search calls by date, caller, intent, outcome
  • Listen to recordings
  • Read transcripts
  • Export data for analysis

Voice includes PII detection and redaction (via ODW.ai Shield):

  • PII detection: Voice scans the conversation for sensitive information (names, phone numbers, health info, credit card numbers)
  • Redaction: Sensitive information is redacted from transcripts and logs (e.g., "John Smith" → [NAME])
  • Routing: If sensitive information is detected, Voice can route the call to a local model only (no cloud API)

For healthcare (HIPAA), legal (privilege), and financial services, Voice ensures sensitive data never leaves your infrastructure unless you explicitly configure it to.

09 · PRICING

Scaling & Operations

7 questions

Voice scales horizontally:

  • SaaS: Unlimited concurrent calls (we handle scaling)
  • Private cloud: Scales with your infrastructure. A single node can handle ~50 concurrent calls. Add more nodes for higher capacity.
  • On-premises: Scales with your infrastructure. Typical deployments handle 50-200 concurrent calls.

For very high-volume deployments (500+ concurrent calls), we recommend Kubernetes with auto-scaling.

If Voice goes down, calls are handled by your telephony provider's failover:

  • Voicemail: Calls go to voicemail (your existing voicemail system)
  • Forwarding: Calls can be forwarded to a backup number (e.g., your mobile, a colleague's number)
  • Queue: Some carriers offer call queuing — callers wait until Voice comes back online

For high-availability deployments, we recommend:

  • SaaS: We provide 99.9% uptime SLA (Enterprise tier)
  • Private cloud: Use multiple replicas across availability zones
  • On-premises: Use failover clustering

11 · ROADMAP

Limitations & Roadmap

8 questions

Voice is powerful, but it has limitations in v1.0:

  • No outbound calling: Voice answers incoming calls but doesn't initiate outbound calls (callbacks, reminders). Planned for v1.1.
  • No video calls: Voice is audio-only. No video component.
  • No contact-center features: Voice is a receptionist, not a full contact-center platform (no ACD queues, agent desktop, workforce management).
  • No custom voice cloning: Use preset voices only. Custom cloning planned for v1.2.
  • No real-time translation: Language is set per-call. No live translation mid-call.
  • No payment processing: Voice can't take credit card numbers over the phone (PCI scope). Planned for v2.0.
  • No conferencing: Single caller + agent only. No multi-party calls.

Outbound calling is planned for v1.1 (Q4 2026). It will include:

  • Callbacks: "We'll call you back at 3pm" → Voice calls the customer at 3pm
  • Appointment reminders: Voice calls customers 24 hours before their appointment
  • Follow-ups: Voice calls leads who requested information

Outbound calling requires careful compliance controls (TCPA in the US, GDPR in Europe) to avoid spam calls. We're building the guardrails first.

Planned for v1.2 (Q1 2027). Custom voice cloning will allow you to create a voice that sounds like a specific person (e.g., your company's spokesperson, a celebrity endorser).

This requires advanced AI models and careful ethical controls (to prevent misuse). We're working with leading TTS providers to offer this feature safely.

Planned for v1.2. Real-time translation will allow Voice to handle callers who switch between languages mid-call (e.g., Spanglish, Franglais).

This is technically challenging (requires real-time language detection and translation) but is a high-priority feature for multilingual markets.

Planned for v2.0 (2027). Payment processing over the phone requires PCI DSS compliance (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard), which is a significant undertaking.

When implemented, Voice will be able to:

  • Collect credit card numbers securely
  • Process payments via integrated payment gateways (Stripe, PayPal, etc.)
  • Send receipts via SMS/email

For now, if a caller needs to make a payment, Voice can transfer to a human or send a secure payment link via SMS.

Key milestones:

  • v1.0 (Q3 2026): MVP launch. Inbound calls, appointment booking, Q&A, call routing, Vault integration, sovereign deployment.
  • v1.1 (Q4 2026): Outbound calling (callbacks, reminders), multi-party warm transfers, native CRM integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot).
  • v1.2 (Q1 2027): Custom voice cloning, real-time translation, sentiment detection and escalation triggers.
  • v2.0 (Q2 2027): Payment processing (PCI-compliant), voice biometrics for caller authentication, proactive outbound campaigns.

Roadmap is subject to change based on customer feedback. Contact us for the latest roadmap or to request features.

Voice is not the right fit if:

  • You need a full contact-center platform: Voice is a receptionist, not a CCaaS replacement. If you need ACD queues, agent desktop, workforce management for 100+ agents, use Genesys, Five9, or NICE.
  • You need outbound marketing campaigns: Voice doesn't do high-volume outbound calling (telemarketing, robocalls). This is ethically and legally problematic.
