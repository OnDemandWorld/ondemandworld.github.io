01 · OVERVIEW

The Basics — What Is Hunt?

9 questions

Hunt is an AI-powered outbound sales co-pilot. It helps small B2B sales teams run outbound at the volume and quality of a much larger team.

Here's how it works:

  1. Identify prospects: AI matches your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP), detects signals (funding, job changes, tech stack changes)
  2. Research prospects: AI generates context-rich prospect profiles (company info, recent news, pain points)
  3. Generate outreach: AI drafts personalized emails, LinkedIn messages, call scripts — grounded in your brand voice and product context
  4. Manage cadences: Multi-step, multi-channel sequences with intelligent timing
  5. Human review: Every outbound message requires human approval before sending

The key difference from other outbound tools: self-hosted, human-in-the-loop, suite-integrated. Your prospect data stays on your infrastructure, and no message goes out without human approval.

Small B2B sales teams face compounding challenges:

  • Volume vs. quality: Outbound at scale demands personalization, but personalization at scale without AI is impossible for small teams
  • Tool sprawl: Teams cobble together 5-8 tools (list building, enrichment, sequencing, CRM, analytics) with brittle integrations
  • Data sovereignty risk: Most outbound tools require sending prospect PII and CRM data to their cloud — creating compliance exposure
  • Compliance complexity: CAN-SPAM, GDPR, LinkedIn ToS are difficult to enforce manually at scale
  • Lack of pipeline integration: Outbound tools operate in isolation from the rest of the revenue pipeline

Hunt solves this by providing self-hosted, human-in-the-loop outbound automation with native pipeline integration.

Hunt is designed for small B2B sales teams (2-10 reps), particularly in regulated industries:

  • SaaS companies: Selling to other businesses
  • Professional services: Consulting, agencies, legal, accounting
  • Manufacturing: B2B industrial sales
  • Healthcare IT: Selling to hospitals, clinics
  • Financial services: Selling to banks, fintech, insurance
  • Any B2B company: That needs to run outbound but can't afford a large sales team

If you're a small sales team trying to punch above your weight, Hunt is for you.

Apollo and Clay are cloud-based outbound tools. Hunt is a self-hosted, human-in-the-loop outbound co-pilot.

Hunt offers three deployment options:

  1. Docker Compose: Easiest. One command (docker-compose up) and you're running. Best for development and small deployments.
  2. Kubernetes (Helm): Production-ready. Helm chart provided. Best for large deployments, high availability, auto-scaling.
  3. Bare metal: For advanced users. Install directly on Linux servers. Maximum control, but requires more setup.

For most customers, Docker Compose is the fastest path. For production, we recommend Kubernetes.

Setup time depends on your deployment:

  • Docker Compose: 30 minutes. Pull images, configure environment variables, run docker-compose up.
  • Kubernetes: 2-4 hours. Deploy Helm chart, configure ingress, set up storage, test.
  • Bare metal: 1-2 days. Install dependencies, configure services, test.

After deployment, you need to:

  • Configure your email provider (Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, SMTP)
  • Import your first prospect list
  • Create your first campaign

Target: <2 hours from signup to first campaign launched.

If Hunt goes down, outbound operations are unavailable, but your data is safe:

  • Data storage: All data is stored in your database (PostgreSQL) — not lost
  • Recovery: Restart Hunt, and it reconnects to your database
  • High availability: For production, deploy Hunt with multiple replicas (Kubernetes)

For high-availability deployments, we recommend:

  • Kubernetes: 3+ replicas across availability zones
  • Managed database: RDS, Cloud SQL, or Azure SQL (99.9% uptime SLA)
  • Load balancer: Distribute traffic across replicas
